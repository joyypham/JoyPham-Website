Studying at the top of tall buildings, walking through campus with a Taylor Swift song in my ears, finding passion in fashion, thrifting, curating new Pinterest boards, decorating for the merry holidays, or taking notes in lecture. All these activities with a boba in hand. That’s my version of ideal and where you’ll catch me on the daily.

I’m a second year college student who is a huge advocate for romanticizing life and finding the daily joys of living. The walks to class, the challenge of learning organic chemistry, and being part of the bustling crowd of people each with their own unique agendas. Realizing each of these daily occurrences is surprisingly comforting and defines what it means to be part of a community.

Building community and fostering self-discovery are top priorities for me. That is why I find thorough enjoyment in my job as a Resident Advisor (RA). In my position as a RA, I have been given the amazing opportunity to promote both community and self-discovery. In the residential dorms, incoming first year students are supported by their upperclassmen. As I am currently a second year, this is my first year as a RA and my second year in Residential Life (ResLife). Throughout my first year, I was an active member of the Residence Hall Association (RHA), specifically part of the programming committee. The RHA team collectively helped to communicate students’ needs and concerns with the building president.
